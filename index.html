The Challenge

First-Line IT Operations Are Expensive to Scale.

IT departments face a structural tension: ticket volumes grow with the business, but staffing costs, skill gaps, and operational inconsistency make linear scaling unsustainable.

Rising Personnel Costs

Each first-line FTE carries a true annual cost of €70,000–€85,000+ when taxes, benefits, and overhead are accounted for.

Increasing Ticket Volumes

Digital operations generate exponentially more support demand. Manual teams cannot absorb this growth without proportional headcount increases.

Talent Shortages

Qualified IT support professionals are increasingly difficult to recruit and retain, creating coverage gaps and SLA risk.

Training Overhead

New hires require weeks of onboarding before reaching operational efficiency. Knowledge walks out the door every time someone leaves.

SLA Pressure

Response time commitments become harder to sustain as volumes peak, shifts end, and attention degrades through repetitive manual work.

Human Inconsistency

Quality varies across shifts, agents, and fatigue levels. Inconsistent first-line handling increases escalation rates and user dissatisfaction.

Limited Operational Hours

Human-only operations leave gaps in after-hours, weekend, and holiday coverage — increasing mean time to resolution for end users.

The structural risk

Without automation, IT cost structures become a drag on digital transformation — not an enabler of it.

Manual first-line operations do not scale with modern IT demand.
